Working Together to Breathe Better Globally

Welcome to Breathe Free, a global initiative dedicated to doing things than tobacco usage. This web application provides information and tools to help individuals lead healthier lives.

Whether you're looking to better your own health, support a loved one in their journey, or simply learn more about the impact of breathing healthy air, this web application is here to help.

By reducing smoking rates, we can save lives, improve public health, decrease healthcare costs, and create a healthier environment for future generations.

Quit Progress Tracker

Set your quit date and track your progress toward better health. See how your body recovers over time and celebrate each milestone.

1
20 minutes: Blood pressure and pulse rate return to normal
2
24 hours: Carbon monoxide is eliminated from the body
3
48 hours: Nerve endings start to regrow, and ability to smell and taste is enhanced
4
72 hours: Bronchial tubes relax, making breathing easier; lung capacity increases
5
2 weeks: Circulation improves, walking becomes easier
6
1 month: Appearance improves, including reduced skin aging and clearer complexion
7
3 months: Lung function improves by 30%, and breathing problems decrease
8
6 months: Less coughing, sinus congestion, fatigue, and shortness of breath
9
1 year: Risk of coronary heart disease is half that of a smoker
10
5 years: Stroke risk is reduced to that of a non-smoker
11
10 years: Risk of lung cancer falls to about half that of a smoker
12
15 years: Risk of heart disease is the same as someone who has never smoked
